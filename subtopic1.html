Cyber Threats in Healthcare

Source 1

URLHHS Ransomware Fact Sheet
AuthorU.S. Department of Health & Human Services
AbstractThe webpage is about how ransomware can severely affect healthcare systems. Ransomware is considered a serious cyber security threat. The website outlines what ransomware is, how cybercriminals can gain access to these symptoms, and what may happen when files are encrypted and inaccessible. It also explains how ransomware attacks actually become a HIPAA violation. This is because patient information becomes exposed or locked. It emphasizes that ransomware does not just disrupt technologu, but also delays medical treatment and operations which could cost a patient's life. The website provides recommendations to avoid this such as backing up data, monitoring networks, properly training employees, and making sure there is an emergency response plan in place.

Source 2

URLASPR Cybersecurity Page
AuthorASPR, U.S. HHS
AbstractThis resource explains how cybersecurity is a form of emergency preparedness for a healthcare sector. Cyberattacks can impact hospitals the same way that natural disasters can interrupt critical services and daily operations. The website provides guidance for healthcare organizations to strengthen their defenses, identity possible risks, and plan ahead in case cyber incidents do occur. It also mentions the growing threat of randomware and data breaches. It stresses that there is a need for coordinated response strategies in this day and age. To sum up, it presents cybersecurity as an essential tool to protecting not just medical technology, but patient safety too.
